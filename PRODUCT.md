# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

High-net-worth individuals, interior designers, architects, and hospitality procurement teams in UAE and GCC. They discover the site on mobile (80%+ of UAE traffic is mobile), but final decisions happen at a desk. They are not browsing — they are assessing. They expect silence: no pop-ups, no chat widgets, no urgency pricing. They come to evaluate craftsmanship and aesthetic judgment before making contact.

## Product Purpose

Parametrika (parametrika.ae) is a luxury parametric furniture catalog for a Dubai-based business (sister brand to New Era Exclusive). It showcases bespoke parametric furniture and decorative products — parametric wood furniture, flexible marble, chandeliers, soft wall panels, smart toilets, and smart dryers. There are no prices anywhere; everything is bespoke and quoted individually. The sole success metric is a qualified lead: a WhatsApp message or a form submission from someone who is ready to engage.

## Brand Personality

Organic, sculptural, warm. The brand draws from natural parametric form, handcrafted quality, CNC precision, and the quiet warmth of wood. Not cold-tech. Not fast-fashion luxury. The feeling is closer to the quiet weight of something made to last — a confidence that does not need to announce itself. Materials and process are the story; the brand voice steps back to let them speak.

## Anti-references

- IKEA and mass-market furniture brands — democratic pricing, bright white layouts, busy category grids, prominent badges and sales
- Real estate and realtor sites — gallery grids, spec tables, "Book a showing" conversion energy
- Aggressive "Arabic luxury" tropes — gold everywhere, ornamental borders, shouting opulence, visual noise
- SaaS and fintech glassmorphism — metric cards, blur panels, dashboard energy, any design that reads as software

## Design Principles

**Let the object speak.** Copy and layout exist to frame the product, not compete with it. Imagery leads; everything else creates space around it.

**Earned access.** The absence of prices is not a wall — it is an invitation. The path to contact should feel like a natural next step for someone who already knows they want to reach out, not a friction point.

**Warmth through restraint.** Luxury is not in how much is added but in what is chosen to leave out. Every element on screen must justify its presence. Whitespace, silence, and stillness are active design decisions.

**Stillness as authority.** Animations and transitions exist to reveal, not to impress. Motion should feel like breathing — slow, intentional, and reduced to nothing when the user asks for it.

**Material honesty.** Typography, color, and texture should echo the materials of the products: natural grain, organic curve, CNC precision. No gradients that suggest nothing, no colors chosen for trend.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum across all pages and locales
- Full RTL layout for the Arabic locale (ar) — not just text direction, but spacing, icon orientation, and component mirroring
- Mobile-first implementation — the majority of UAE traffic arrives on mobile; the experience must be complete and considered at small viewports, not an afterthought
- Reduced-motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion` — all scroll-driven animations and transitions must have a static fallback
- No color-only information — critical states (errors, active selection, status) must be communicated through shape, label, or pattern in addition to color
